Our starting hypothesis

Market entry barriers are often institutional, not only regulatory

Our preliminary view is that ICT firms in emerging digital markets face barriers created by formal rules, implementation uncertainty, institutional coordination gaps, and political economy pressures.

Rather than producing a legal inventory, the project could compare how regulatory systems operate in practice and identify where policy design, implementation or stakeholder coordination may affect digital market development.

Preliminary research question

What regulatory, institutional and political economy barriers do ICT firms face when entering or expanding in high-growth emerging digital markets — and what policy options could reduce these barriers while supporting digital development goals?

To be refined with GSMA

Analytical framework

A structured lens for comparing regulatory environments

We propose an initial four-part framework that can be adjusted based on GSMA’s priorities and the intended use of the final output.

1. Regulatory architecture

Spectrum licensing, telecom authorization, data governance, cybersecurity and security review frameworks.

2. Institutional actors

Regulators, ministries, enforcement bodies, industry associations and the coordination mechanisms among them.

3. Political economy

Industrial policy, localization pressures, procurement preferences, strategic sectors and geopolitical considerations.

4. Policy gaps

Misalignment between policy objectives and firms’ operational needs, including uncertainty, compliance burden and implementation risk.
